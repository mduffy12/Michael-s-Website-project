Penguins

Penguins cannot fly; instead, they use their wings as flippers to help them swim underwater. Penguins are great swimmers and divers. Their eyes help them see even in the darkest water, but they cannot see well when they are out of the water.

The Two Royals

When we think of penguins we think of royalty. So, it is no surprise that the two largest penguins are named the emperer and the king.

Emperor Penguin

Emperor Penguins are the largest penguins in the family. Some reaching heights of over 3 ft tall and weighing almost 99 pounds. Their diet consists of fish but also includes crustaceans, such as krill and squid. While hunting, the species can be submurged up to 20 minutes, diving to a depth of 1,755 ft.

King Penguin

King penguins are the second largest species of penguins, smaller, but somewhat similar in apperance to the emperor penguin. They are found in the South Atlantic. King penguins look and behave like emperor penguins. They have longer and less curved beaks, while emperor penguins have shorter and more curved beaks.
