Penguins

Penguins cannot fly; instead, they use their wings as flippers to help them swim underwater. Penguins are great swimmers and divers. Their eyes help them see even in the darkest water, but they cannot see well when they are out of the water.

The Two Royals

When we think of penguins we think of royality. So it is no surprise that the two largest penguins are named the Emporer and King.

Emporer Penguin

Emporer Penguins are the biggest penguins in the family.Some reaching heights of over 3 ft tall and weighing almost 99 pounds.Their diet consist of fish but also includes crustaceans, such as krill and squid. While hunting the species can be submurged around 20 minutes diving to a depth of 1,755 ft.

King Penguin

King Penguins are the second largest species of penguins, smaller, but some what similar in apperance to the Emporer Penguin. They are found in the South Atlantic. King Penguins look and behave like Emporer Penguins...King Penguins have longer and less curved beaks, while Emporer Penguins have shorter and more curved beaks.
